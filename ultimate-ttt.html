How it works
This whole game is one self-contained HTML file — there's no game server, and nothing ever stores or runs the game but the two phones themselves. Create a game and you get a short code; your friend enters it and your phones link up. During the few seconds of setup, a small free relay (a Firebase database) helps the two phones find each other — like swapping numbers through a mutual friend — and then it's discarded. After that your moves travel directly phone-to-phone over an encrypted WebRTC channel. If a strict or mobile network blocks a direct link, WebRTC quietly routes that same encrypted data through a relay so you can still play — still just the two of you, no server ever seeing the game. Prefer no relay at all? “Connect manually instead” trades the codes by hand. Drop mid-game? Your board is saved and re-syncs automatically when you reconnect.
